Most of the time, the root cause of this issue is poor brightness/contrast settings. In general, OCT imaging is delicate, and the settings can be skewed even without direct intervention from the operators (e.g. from power fluctuations).

To resolve:

  • In the OCT Scan section of the screen (right side of the dividing line, toward the bottom near the middle of the screen) there should be a button with an icon resembling either a "magic wand" or a "half-eclipsed sun," depending on scan type and software version. These are the brightness/contrast settings. Click the button
  • In the brightness/contrast control panel, toward the bottom there should be another button with an icon displaying a leftward-looping arrow, indicating "revert." This restores the brightness and contrast settings to their factory defaults. Click this button
  • Close the Brightness/Contrast control panel
  • Verify the OCT scan preview windows are now showing the expected visual data
