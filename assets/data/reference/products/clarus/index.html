Clarus Reference Index

Description

Clarus is an ultra-wide field (UWF) fundus camera. It will capture and stitch together (montage) 2 wide-field images to create the UWF Picture. This UWF image shows more area of the fundus than a Visucam can capture in seven or more images.

The Clarus is "non-mydriatic" meaning, in most cases, pupil dilation is not required to get quality color images of the fundus. However, Fluorescein Angiography (FA) will always require pupil dilation.
