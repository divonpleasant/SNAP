Description

Atlas Corneal Topographers capture a "topographical map" of the cornea being measured. This map shows surface irregularities, thinning and distortions of the cornea. The device is typically used for contact lens fitting and for pre- and post-operation corneal procedures, including refractive surgery and corneal transplant.

The device projects "placido" rings onto the patient's cornea and then measures the captured image for spacing between rings, distortions and other factors to determine the corneal topography.
